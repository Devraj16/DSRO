DSRO’s Voyage to the Future

2023 – DSRO Was Born

Devraj’s Space Research Organization was officially founded with the vision of pioneering space innovation.
Our goal: make space technology more accessible, efficient, and sustainable.
We laid down the foundation of our labs, recruited core talent, and defined our long-term strategy.
This was the beginning of a bold journey toward the stars.

2024 — First Satellite Launched

DSRO successfully launched its first satellite, DS-1, into low-earth orbit.
This satellite focused on earth observation and weather data collection.
It marked our first real-world milestone in the aerospace sector.
Partnerships with universities and startups grew significantly this year.

2025 — Reusable Rocket Launch

We achieved a major breakthrough with the testing of our first reusable rocket system.
The rocket launched and landed vertically, cutting mission costs by 60%.
Our R&D team began building modular launch vehicles for multi-purpose missions.
DSRO gained international recognition for its low-cost, sustainable approach.

2026 — Deep Space Program

We initiated our Deep Space Program focused on interplanetary missions.
The goal: explore Mars orbit, asteroid belts, and long-duration satellite operations.
Collaboration with global space agencies brought new scientific insight.
This year marked DSRO’s transition from low-earth to deep space ambitions.

2027 — Quantum Tech in Space

We deployed quantum communication payloads enabling ultra-secure satellite links.
DSRO began working on real-time data sharing between Earth and orbital systems.
Space-grade quantum computing prototypes were introduced in our labs.
It positioned DSRO as a leader in next-gen aerospace tech.
